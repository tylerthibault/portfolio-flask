As a night job, I work a couple of times a week at a local business called Okandogs . While there, I noticed that their website was outdated and lacked the modern features that could help improve their online presence. I thought that building them a mock website would be a great way to showcase my skills and potentially help their business. So, in my free time, I created a website for them with all the latest features and design elements. However, after presenting it to the business owners, they decided not to use it, which was disappointing. Nonetheless, I am proud of the effort and time that I put into the project, and it was a great opportunity to practice my web development skills.

Okandogs, a non-profit organization located in Cashmere, Washington, was established by Jan and Tom Short in 2014. Their mission is to provide medical care, spay/neuter services, and adoptions for dogs in need across the state, with a commitment to achieving a 100% adoption rate and zero euthanasia/fatality rate. The shelter has taken in and rescued hundreds of dogs, earning a positive reputation within the local community. Last year, Okandogs took in 801 dogs. One of the organization's success stories is Tucker, a four-year-old Labrador-Retriever mix who was surrendered by his owner earlier this year. Okandogs provided him with medical care, and just a few months later, Tucker was adopted by Major League Baseball's Seattle Mariners franchise as its clubhouse pet, a heartwarming example of Okandogs' dedication to giving dogs a second chance at life and finding them loving forever homes.
